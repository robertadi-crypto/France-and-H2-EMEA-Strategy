The yearly strategy cadence

Three nested rhythms — aligned from annual direction to 6-week execution

1.
Bi-yearly Strategy Day
Sets the strategy to reach the BHAG (Big Hairy Audacious Goals). Happens at the start of H1 and H2.
2.
Quarterly Cycle
Check-in on the strategy — retrospective on bets and planning next steps. Keeps the 6-month direction honest.
3.
6-Week Sprints
The execution layer. 8 sprints per year, two per quarter. Each sprint maps to the strategic bets set at the Strategy Day.

02
France — Sales
QBR Insights · Beliefs & Bets · Strategic Initiatives

What we learned in QBRs — France Sales

Where growth is happening
  • CRM outperforming at 143% of target ($0.86M); SE-paired deals delivered $305K
  • Renewals + expansion is the most reliable line (SCC, Ceva, Imperva)
  • Outbound gaining: OBD 2.0 generated $541K pipe in 4 blitz days; NB +28% YoY
  • Partner-Led resilient: 86% attainment vs. 69% Direct
  • Public Sector seeded: Puy de Dôme + CH Chalon
Where growth is stalling
  • AI attach dramatically underweighted: $0.15M in Q1 despite 3 of 5 Big Bets being AI-framed
  • Dev (42%) and Service (41%) lagging — multi-product story is CRM-only
  • "Missing middle": 0 reps at 80-99%, ~50% below 50%; 3 reps drove 41.6% of Q1 closed won
  • Events PG broken: 6 cancelled; ENT hiring 1/3 roles; Q1 buffer -$16K
  • Leadership vacuum: 0 of 2 management slots filled
Customer behaviour shifting
  • Churn is adoption-driven, not feature-driven (Fresenius, Pierre Fabre: "low usage")
  • RFP volume in ENT at all-time high (Sanofi, Veolia $150K)
  • Multi-house expansion emerging: Kering → Gucci + Boucheron under one MSA
  • Legal flexibility asks: Crédit Agricole, Indosuez data residency, Public Sector
What's working / not working
Working: Mission March, CRM co-sell, SE-paired XL deals, OBD 2.0, weekly PG cadences, public sector focus

Not working: Event reliance · Leadership vacuum · AI attach · Mid-tier development · SFDC hygiene · Exec sponsor coverage for XL accounts

03
France — Partners
QBR Insights · Beliefs & Bets · Strategic Initiatives

What we learned in QBRs — France Partners

Where growth is happening
  • Marine Thouvenin: 130% attainment, 103% PL, $350K closed — pulled the whole team's Q1
  • Elina Bernard building increasing trend (86% PL, $821K PG in March alone)
  • PL pipeline generation accelerating: $778K in January → $1.83M in March
Where growth is stalling
  • Bertrand Duchesne: 52% attainment, 61% PL, flat across all three months — no recovery trend
  • France Q1 overall at 71% — dragged by underperformance
  • Synolia (7-year partner) in termination process; Seidor FR on Partner PIP
  • Q2 PL commit ($1.29M) covers only ~87.2% of the $1.48M target
Customer behaviour shifting
  • Customers and partners want deeper engagement — CPM roadshow and S&P collaboration days being introduced
  • Deals growing in complexity: big deal focus on MP & AI requires CPM-led, solution-oriented conversations
What's working / not working
Working: Marine's recovery validates CPM-led deal control. Elina's foundations strong. Team PG volume strong.

Not working: Bertrand unchanged after 11 months · Partner portfolio hygiene poor · Synolia exit overdue · CPM deal involvement inconsistent · Devoteam under-delivering

Strategic Bets — France Partners

Belief that led to each bet · Hero metric · All new for H2

New for H2
Partner Capability over Coverage
Because the ecosystem needs more depth — Synolia's exit is misalignment; capability must lead coverage
Exit Synolia; resolve Seidor PIP; recruit technically capable partners
Hero metric
Partner-led attainment as standalone metric
New for H2
CPM Execution Quality as Growth Lever
Because the constraint is consistency of CPM-led execution — previously CPMs were more customer-focused
Victor K as CP Team Lead; clear performance standards with defined consequences
Hero metric
All CPMs above 85% attainment by Q3
New for H2
Integrated Sales & Partners Motion
Because PL covers ~82% of target — direct and partners operating in silos is part of the problem
Joint pipeline review cadence; partner days as activation mechanisms
Hero metric
Partner-sourced pipeline as % of total France pipeline, growing MoM
New for H2
AI as Commercial Capability
Because AI is shifting from differentiator to qualification criterion — partners who can't lead AI conversations will lose deals
Enablement content + AI certification; align with central product marketing
Hero metric
% of France partner deals with AI component by H2
New for H2
Enterprise & Mid-Market GTM Reposition
Because France is under-penetrated on larger deals — Q2 has explicit big deal focus but SMB still unpenetrated
Account segmentation for highest expansion potential; CPM time toward strategic accounts
Hero metric
Average deal size growing QoQ through H2
